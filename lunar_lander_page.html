Lunar Lander Reinforcement Learning

Project Overview & Model Architecture

Developed a Deep Q-Network (DQN) based reinforcement learning model for solving the Lunar Lander problem in OpenAI Gym. The agent learns to stabilize descent while minimizing fuel consumption, balancing between reward-driven learning and policy optimization.

The environment dynamics include:

  • Gravity, wind, and turbulence effects.
  • Thruster-based control for vertical and lateral movement.
  • Reward system encouraging smooth landing and penalizing crashes.

Results & Insights

- Achieved stable landing in 95% of test cases after training.

- Fine-tuned hyperparameters improved convergence by 40%.

- Policy adaptation under stochastic wind disturbances.

- Comparison: Q-learning struggled with instability, whereas DQN successfully learned optimal landing strategies.
