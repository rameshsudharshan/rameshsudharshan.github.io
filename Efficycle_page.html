SAE Efficycle

Project Overview

Designed and manufactured an Efficycle as part of a team of 8, a hybrid electric + human-powered three-wheeled vehicle. The project involved mechanical design, structural analysis, and powertrain optimization to achieve high efficiency and performance.

Key features include:

  • Hybrid drive system combining electric and manual pedaling.
  • Lightweight tubular chassis for optimized strength-to-weight ratio.
  • Regenerative braking system for energy recovery.
  • Optimized aerodynamics for minimal drag.

Structural Analysis - Frontal Impact

- Weight of trike: 250 kg

- Speed before collision: 9 m/s

- The simulation revealed high-stress areas, allowing reinforcement for safety.

Structural Analysis - Side Impact

- The same conditions were applied as frontal impact.

- The structure effectively absorbs lateral forces, ensuring minimal deformation.

Structural Analysis - Rollover

- The top frame was tested for rollover durability.

- Impact force applied to top members while one side was fixed.

- The structure remained stable under simulated roll conditions.

Brake Deformation Analysis

- Maximum applied force: 50N

- The brake rotor exhibited minimal deformation under braking force.

Steering Mechanism

- Steering ratio: 0.8

- Turning radius: 1.87 m

- Track width: 43 inches

- Wheelbase: 39 inches

The steering mechanism ensures a tight turning radius while maintaining stability.
